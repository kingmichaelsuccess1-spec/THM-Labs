# TryHackMe — Humans as Attack Vectors

## Overview
**Platform:** TryHackMe
**Room:** Humans as Attack Vectors
**Category:** Social Engineering / Human-Centric Attacks
**Analyst Focus:** SOC Tier 1 — Detection & Prevention

## Description
This room explores one of the most persistent and dangerous vulnerabilities in cybersecurity — the human element. It covers how and why attackers target people, and equips SOC analysts with the knowledge to detect and prevent these attacks before they result in a breach.

## Key Concepts Covered
- Why humans are the weakest link in the security chain
- Attack techniques: phishing, pretexting, baiting, and social engineering
- Attacker motivations and target profiling
- Detection strategies for social engineering at the SOC level
- Prevention and security awareness measures

## SOC Analyst Takeaways
- Detection: Recognizing phishing indicators and suspicious email patterns
- Prevention: Security awareness and least privilege principles
- Response: Escalation procedures for suspected human-vector attacks

## Skills Demonstrated
- Social engineering attack lifecycle knowledge
- Triage of human-targeted attack indicators
- Attacker psychology and targeting methodology
- Detection and prevention application in SOC context
